Exam & Question Strategy

Understanding the mechanics of the exam is as important as knowing the syllabus. This section details the different question types you'll face and the marking scheme. Mastering these rules allows you to make smart decisions during the exam, maximizing your score and minimizing penalties.

Question Typology

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

One correct answer out of four options. These have negative marking (1/3 for 1-mark, 2/3 for 2-mark questions). Strategy: Use elimination to increase your chances. Avoid guessing wildly.

Multiple Select Questions (MSQs)

One or more correct answers out of four options. You must select ALL correct options for credit. No negative marking and no partial credit. Strategy: Treat each option as a true/false statement. Always attempt these questions.

Numerical Answer Type (NATs)

Enter a real number answer using the virtual keyboard. No negative marking. Strategy: These test direct application. Always attempt them, even if you have to make an educated guess on your calculation.

The Golden Rule of Attempting

The most critical strategic insight is this: You must attempt every single MSQ and NAT question. There is zero penalty for a wrong answer on these types, meaning you can only gain marks. Leaving an MSQ or NAT question blank is a strategic error that can cost you valuable rank-boosting points. Be cautious with MCQs, but be aggressive with MSQs and NATs.
